When do Google Ads make sense?

There are a few conditions, which we look at together in the first consultation, that you should be aware of. They determine how successful Google Ads can actually be:

  • Click prices are usually between one and ten francs. With suitable unit prices, high sales volume or a high customer lifetime value, this calculation usually works out positively. For low-priced impulse purchases, social media ads are often the better choice.
  • Google Ads only bring people to your site. It's only there that it's decided whether a conversion - e.g. a purchase or an enquiry - actually happens. That means usability, transparency and conversion optimisation play a big part in success.

You don't need to worry about anything else at this stage. With me as your SEA freelancer, you get everything from a single source, from campaign setup to conversion tracking. And, as the person who looks after your campaign from setup to evaluation, I can usually give you an individual assessment as early as the first consultation.

Real-world examples

The business offers B2C solutions with longer decision cycles and personal consultation as a central part of the sales process. Because of the limited catchment area and the high order values, the focus wasn't on generating as many enquiries as possible, but on generating qualified consultation appointments.

Implementation

  • Search campaign focused on purchase-ready search queries
  • Geographic restriction to the relevant catchment area
  • Direct appointment booking via an online booking system
  • Average ad budget: CHF 20 per day

Result (12 months)

  • 121 booked first consultations
  • Approx. CHF 60 ad cost per appointment booking

The company sells custom solutions in the B2B sector with a high volume per order. The goal was to generate qualified quote requests via Google Ads.

Implementation

  • Search campaigns on transaction-oriented keywords
  • Focus on search queries with concrete procurement intent
  • Average ad budget: CHF 30 per day

Result (12 months)

  • 173 quote requests
  • Approx. CHF 65 ad cost per request

By the way: if you prefer a more budget-friendly variant of SEA, I have good news at least for the B2B space. Similar to Google Ads, Microsoft Advertising also lets you run ads on Bing.

The market share is smaller, but at least on business devices, where the default search engine is usually preset, it's worthwhile. In my experience, click prices on Bing Ads run 10-20% below Google.
