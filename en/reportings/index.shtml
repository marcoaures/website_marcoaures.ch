A reporting is quickly created: most tools spit out numbers at the push of a button. But a table full of sessions, bounce rates and conversions isn't an insight yet. The real work begins where someone reads the numbers, puts them in the context of your business and judges what's relevant and what's noise.

Collecting data is one thing, understanding it another

Data only shows you that something happened, not why it matters, what you should do about it, or where there's still potential. That translation is exactly my job: I dig deep into your data, depending on the focus from visibility and content analysis to full user journeys, and derive a clear picture from it.

When I see a problem, I note it down and, at the end, sort my findings by what has the most impact. So you don't get twenty equally weighted observations, but an order: what comes first, what's urgent and what can wait. That way, after the meeting you not only know how your numbers stand, but also where you should start.

By the way, you decide the focus. I do complete analyses across all channels or partial reportings, e.g. just « content & visibility » or « conversion paths ». What makes sense in your case we can agree on together.

Why a close look pays off

The most valuable insights rarely appear in big letters on a dashboard: with one client, I noticed in a quarterly reporting that around 4.5% of users hit an error on a lead form. At first glance unremarkable, and with low user numbers not very alarming either, which is why the problem had gone unnoticed for a long time.

The decisive factor here, however, was the context. The company had just planned a larger campaign that would have led to exactly this lead form. Extrapolated to the planned ad budget, a higher four-figure amount would have been wasted if every 23rd submission had been impossible due to an error.

The error, by the way, was a trifle, a required field that wasn't displayed correctly on certain screen sizes. In the end, the insight from the reporting « saved » more ad budget than the entire analysis had cost.
