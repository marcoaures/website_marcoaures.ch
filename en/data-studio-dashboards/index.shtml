Google Data Studio Dashboards

So your team always finds the numbers it regularly needs, up to date and in one place.

In many companies the same exercise repeats itself: someone manually exports numbers from Google Analytics, Google Ads and Search Console, copies them into a spreadsheet and builds a report out of them. And that quarterly, monthly or sometimes even weekly. It costs time every time, is error-prone, and in the worst case the data is already outdated again by the time the recipient reads it.

A dashboard solves exactly that problem: I'd be happy to build you a custom marketing cockpit in Google Data Studio that brings together your most important data sources and keeps them automatically up to date. Your marketing team, the management or a specialist unit can find the charts and metrics they need in it at any time, without anyone having to compile them by hand first.

Who it's for

For teams that regularly need the same data in an orderly form and want to move their reporting from manual copy-pasting to an automated, always up-to-date dashboard.

What you get

A tailor-made dashboard in Google Data Studio that brings your metrics together and updates automatically. In your design and structured so that even non-analysts understand it.

How I work

Individual and aligned with your requirements: I work from your goals, and if something doesn't fit, the dashboard can be modified or extended at any time.

At its core, a dashboard is a single, always up-to-date answer to a recurring question: « How are things going right now? » Instead of compiling that answer anew every month, you set it up cleanly once and after that it updates itself.

What makes a good dashboard

The art, however, rarely lies in packing as many numbers as possible onto one screen. Sure, Google Data Studio can tap into practically any data source and display any number of charts, but that's exactly where the trap is: a dashboard that shows everything ends up answering nothing. It only becomes valuable once it's tailored to the questions your team actually asks.

That's why I don't start with the data, but with you: who looks at the dashboard, how often and with what question in mind? A member of management who wants to see the rough trend once a month needs a different view than a marketing lead steering campaigns weekly. From those requirements I build one view (or several, tailored to the audience) that shows the right metrics clearly and understandably, so that even someone without an analytics background grasps them at a glance.

After that I connect the necessary data sources, from Google Analytics through Google Ads and Search Console to other tools you have in use. The dashboard then keeps itself up to date, and you get an onboarding so you can use it on your own.
