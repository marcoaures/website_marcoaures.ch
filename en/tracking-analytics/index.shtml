Tracking & Analytics

Clean website tracking & custom events you can trust: privacy-compliant, precise and traceable.

Tracking is complex, multi-layered and usually underestimated: from the marketing lead through information security and legal to IT, there are many points of contact and everyone has different requirements and ideas. On top of that, tracking setups are individual from company to company and usually only become "important" once necessary data suddenly turns out to be missing.

It doesn't have to be that way: Tracking & Analytics are my specialty, and as a freelancer I set up your Google Tag Manager and your GA4 so that even the most critical stakeholders give you the green light.

Who it's for

For businesses that value data-driven marketing. Whether for the first clean setup or for overhauling an existing one.

What you get

Implementation or revision of your tracking setup. GA4, Google Tag Manager, social media pixels, custom events and consent management. Including documentation.

How I work

Holistically: I understand tech & code, requirements around consent management & revised Swiss FADP/GDPR compliance, and also which data matters to the marketing team.

Admittedly, a small website that mainly serves as a "shop window" for a small business probably gets by with a mini setup or no analytics at all. But at the latest once the website delivers a measurable share of business success, more marketing budget is meant to flow, management or the board demands a report, or you want to improve your site based on data, clean data is no longer a "luxury" but a minimum requirement.

When analytics gets complex

The crux: setting up simple analytics is actually relatively easy, but more complex setups aren't. As soon as user consent, event listeners, custom events or the interplay of several endpoints (Meta Pixel, Google Ads Conversions, GA4, Looker Studio) come into play, it takes interdisciplinary know-how. There's no point in being able to track everything but not knowing what you're allowed to track. Just as little does a technically perfect tracking concept help if marketing can't use it to answer where the funnel breaks. And even if everything is in place: tracking has to be maintained, otherwise the next website relaunch destroys the setup - usually unnoticed.

These are exactly the problems I can solve for you as a tracking freelancer: I translate marketing requirements into clean GTM setups, mediate between IT and marketing (or build the tech myself right away). From the pixel to the CRM, I know which data needs to land where – and where better not. Consent, revised Swiss FADP and GDPR aren't add-ons with me, but part of the setup. On top of that, I document everything cleanly, so that even in two years you still know what happens where and don't have to start from scratch every time the website is revised.
