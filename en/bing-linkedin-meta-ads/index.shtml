Single campaigns on individual channels

Not every ad needs a multi-channel concept. Often a targeted campaign on a single channel is exactly the right thing:

  • Bing Ads work almost the same as Google Ads technically and can be implemented with manageable extra effort. The market share is smaller, but on business devices Bing is often the preset search engine, and click prices are usually below those of Google.
  • LinkedIn is suitable when you want to reach a clearly defined professional audience, but it's comparatively expensive.
  • Meta is great for reach and visual appeal in the B2C space. Meta also works excellently for « entry-level offers » (e.g. to get people to subscribe to your newsletter so they become customers later).

By the way, LinkedIn and Meta in particular are a good fit for time-limited campaigns around a specific occasion or offer.

Campaigns across multiple channels

If you advertise across multiple paid channels, I take care of coordinating the whole thing. The individual channels each take on different tasks: one brings new people to your site, the other uses retargeting to re-engage those who have already shown interest. That way, someone who came to your website via Google search can be reached again later on Meta.

For this to work, the right tracking is needed in the background: the pixels of the individual platforms, integrated correctly and in line with consent and data protection. That's my field of expertise anyway, which is why the technical foundation for cross-channel campaigns is part of my offering.
