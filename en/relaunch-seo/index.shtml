Relaunch SEO

So your website relaunch doesn't quietly cost you the visibility you've built up.

A relaunch or a larger change to a website often changes more than you're aware of: backlinks suddenly point nowhere, the link structure gets turned upside down, and Google often keeps old pages « in mind » for quite a while. Without clean preparation, you quickly lose rankings that you've built up over years, and in the worst case the website ends up worse off than before the overhaul. And often this only becomes noticeable weeks later, once traffic has already declined.

That's exactly why SEO needs to be considered already during the website project, not just for damage control afterwards: when it comes to relaunches, as your SEO freelancer I do the website sign-off before the new site goes live, check the new architecture, test the redirects, and make sure the move takes your existing visibility along instead of leaving it behind.

Who it's for

For businesses making fundamental changes to their website. And for web agencies that don't offer SEO and tracking themselves and are looking for a reliable partner for their project.

What you get

An SEO sign-off as a fixed part of your relaunch. Checking the site against best practices, redirects etc. and (on request) the clean migration of your tracking.

How I work

Involved rather than tacked on at the end: I work directly with you or your web agency, understand both tech and marketing, and make sure SEO doesn't only become a topic after go-live.

During a relaunch, the focus understandably points forward: new design, new content, new technology. It's easy to lose sight of what the old site could already do. Working URLs, existing rankings and the trust built up with Google over years. Carrying all of that through the move only works if you pay attention to it early enough.

What the SEO sign-off delivers

Before you move into a house, you have it professionally inspected. With a website relaunch, this « sign-off » often falls by the wayside, even though the consequences can be similarly expensive. This is exactly where my relaunch SEO service comes in: I look at the new site before it's live and check typical stumbling blocks before they become a problem. Among other things, this includes:

  • mapping and testing the redirects, so that neither visitors nor crawlers end up running into nothing,
  • making sure canonicals, sitemaps and metadata survive the move and can be processed by Google without obstacles, so that Google « understands » the new website right away,
  • and making sure valuable backlinks and page authority aren't lost because the signals aren't correctly passed on to the new site.

The result is a clear assessment: green light for go-live, or a concrete list of what still needs to be done beforehand.

Why tracking should be part of it too

One point that classic SEO usually doesn't cover, but which can easily be part of the project with me: a relaunch almost always destroys the tracking setup. Tags fire into nothing, custom events reference elements that no longer exist, and in the end exactly the data is missing that you'd want to use at some point to check whether the new website achieved anything.

That's why I offer migrating your tracking as part of my relaunch SEO service: GA4, Google Tag Manager, pixel setups and your events move across too, instead of being patched ad-hoc after go-live, once the damage is already done.

By the way, if the tracking is being touched during the relaunch anyway, that can often be a good opportunity to overhaul the tracking completely: if your new website has new interaction options, audiences or user paths, it's worth combining the website relaunch with a tracking relaunch, so that afterwards everything fits together cleanly.
