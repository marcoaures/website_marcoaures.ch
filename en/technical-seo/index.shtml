Technical SEO & SEO Audits

You're already doing SEO but suspect potential is being left on the table? Often it's not your texts, but what happens "under the hood".

Most businesses that aren't getting anywhere with SEO often hear first: "You need more content." Often that's true. But sometimes the actual problem lies somewhere completely different: in a sitemap Google can't read, in missing heading levels, in internal linking that buries important pages, or in missing structured data that would help search and AI systems classify your content better.

That's exactly where I come in as your freelancer for technical SEO: I look at the "hidden" parts of your website that your visitors never see, but Google and LLMs very much do. After that, I tell you where visibility is probably being lost and what can be done about it.

Who it's for

For businesses that already do SEO and notice something isn't adding up, or who want an independent, deep look at the technical state of their website instead of just hearing "more texts".

What you get

A deep SEO audit with prioritised recommendations for action, or targeted individual services like structured data, crawling optimisation or LLM discoverability. Clearly documented and directly actionable.

How I work

I work independently and transparently. You get clear findings, understandable priorities and concrete next steps, so you can decide yourself what you implement in-house, with your agency or with me.

Technical SEO is everything that determines whether Google, and increasingly AI systems too, can even cleanly capture, understand and classify your website at all, before content even comes into play: the machine readability and the effort needed for it. If this foundation crumbles, even the best text helps little: Google doesn't rank what it can't interpret correctly.

Sure, content is important. But "more blogs" is sometimes the simple answer to a problem that's more complicated. If Google doesn't even index important subpages on your website, if the Search Console spits out warnings and errors, or if important changes are recognised by Google late or not at all, the problem usually lies on the technical side. And in these cases, it doesn't matter whether the problem has always been there, crept in due to an error during a relaunch, or whether a core update changed the mechanics: In such cases, more content mainly creates additional pages with the same unsolved problems. My field of expertise is exactly that: the technical basis for SEO, so that your content reaches the position it deserves.

From audit to implementation

The starting point is usually an audit. I check your website thoroughly and by hand: indexing, crawling and Core Web Vitals. From metadata, headline structure and internal linking to structured data and backlink profile. In the end you get a list of measures, what can be improved on your website, without you having to write 20 new pages.
